As per her career, she began her career as an author and published her first book “The Dorm Room Diet” in 2007 which covers how to develop healthy habits while in college. This book was publicized by multiple media outlets including The Washington Post, Reader’s Digest, Teen Vogue, The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, People, Cosmo Girl! and more.

Apart from that, she also made promotional appearances on Fox & Friends, The Nate Berkus Show, Good Morning America, her father’s program, The Dr. Oz Show, and The Tyra Banks Show. In 2012, she collaborated with the cast of “The Chew” to author “The Chew: Food. Life. Fun.”

She is also the author of “Relish: An Adventure in Food, Style, and Everyday Fun”, the New York Times bestselling. This book contains recipes, tips, relationships, and career advice. Additionally, she wrote articles for The Huffington Post and Glamour. She wrote five articles for a column called “Food for Thought” in 2012.

In addition to this, she also featured on television shows such as Fox & Friends, The Dr. Oz Show, The Rachael Ray Show, Dayside, The Chew, The View, Beat Bobby Flay, Good Morning America, and Good Day New York. She worked as a judge on the Food competition show Cooks vs. Cons.

Reflecting on her personal life, she tied the marriage knot in 2010 with John Jovanovic at the municipal Marriage Bureau in Manhattan. The marriage was done in two religious ceremonies, first at Synaxis of Serbian Saints Church and the other one in Cumberland Foreside, Maine, at the summer home of Daphne Oz’s maternal grandparents.

The lovely couple was blessed with two daughters named Bijou Jovanovic, Domenica Celine Jovanovic, and a son Jovan.
