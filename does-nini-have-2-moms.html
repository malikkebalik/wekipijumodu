Does Nini Have 2 Moms?

September 2024 · 3 minute read

TV

Nini had a romance of her own, but this character has two parents who sometimes make an appearance in 'High School Musical: The Musical: The Series.'

Published on July 31, 2021

2 min read

High School Musical: The Musical: The Series featured romances between Seb and Carlos and, briefly, Nini and EJ. There are also some relationships between the parents of these East High School students. 

Here’s what we know about Nini’s parents from this Disney+ original series. 

Nini is 1 character from the Disney+ original series, ‘High School Musical: The Musical: The Series’

Of course, there was drama between Nini and Ricky during High School Musical: The Musical: The Series. However, there is much more to this character portrayed by Olivia Rodrigo. This character showcased her love for theater with her role in High School Musical and later her enrollment in a performing arts high school. 

She also wrote and recorded original songs from social media, earning plenty of fans in the process. For some episodes, fans met members of Nini’s family. That includes her grandmother and her two moms. 

Nini does have 2 moms in this Disney+ original series

Fans do see a glimpse of this character outside of East High School. In season 1 of the Disney+ original, High School Musical: The Musical: The Series, viewers met Carol and Dana, who are Nini’s parents. 

“Yup, two moms,” Olivia said at the series premiere during an interview with Hollywood Life. “My character has two moms, and I loved the way that they did that. They just portrayed it as completely normal and natural, which… that’s just real life.” 

“And it’s not a statement. It’s not a punchline; it’s just how it is,” she continued. “It’s completely accepted and just a normal part of life that we’re portraying.”

‘High School Musical: The Musical: The Series’ also included characters Carlos and Seb

With the “Homecoming” episode of season 1 and the quinceañero episode of season 2, fans caught a glimpse into the relationship between Carlos and Seb, two theater students at East High School. 

According to The Salt Lake Tribune, Carlos is only the second gay regular in a Disney series; the first was Cyrus (Joshua Rush) in Andi Mack. 

“There was a flood of messages that were, like, ‘Wow, this is great. I feel seen. You’re telling my story,’” Frankie Rodriguez, who portrays the character, said during an interview with the same publication. “But it was mainly from adults who reached out and said, ‘If I would have seen this when I was in high school, I would have felt a lot less alone.’

Carlos and Seb are two central characters in this mockumentary. Disney+ also created the LGBTQ characters named Camila and Bobby for their original series Diary of a Future President.

Episodes of High School Musical: The Musical: The Series are available for streaming exclusively on Disney+. To learn more about this subscription service, visit their website.
